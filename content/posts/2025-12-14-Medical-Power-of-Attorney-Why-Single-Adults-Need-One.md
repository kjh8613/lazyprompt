```markdown
# Role
Senior Legal Writer specializing in estate planning and healthcare law.

# Context
Many individuals, especially young adults over 18, are unaware that their parents no longer automatically have the right to make medical decisions for them in the event of an emergency or incapacitation. This can lead to significant legal and emotional complications during critical healthcare situations. This guide aims to address this knowledge gap and advocate for proactive legal preparedness.

# Task
Write a legal preparedness guide titled "Medical Power of Attorney: Why Single Adults Need One". The guide should:

1.  Start with a compelling introduction highlighting the importance of medical decision-making autonomy for adults.
2.  Explain clearly and concisely that parents lose the legal right to make medical decisions for their children once they turn 18, regardless of the parent-child relationship. Use a relatable example to illustrate this point.
3.  Define "Medical Power of Attorney" (POA) in plain language, explaining its purpose and functionality.
4.  Outline the key benefits of having a Medical POA, including:
    *   Ensuring your healthcare wishes are respected.
    *   Designating a trusted individual to make decisions on your behalf.
    *   Avoiding potential family conflicts during stressful times.
    *   Streamlining the medical decision-making process.
5.  Provide a step-by-step guide on how to obtain a Medical POA, including:
    *   Researching state-specific requirements.
    *   Finding a suitable POA form (online resources, legal professionals).
    *   Completing the form accurately.
    *   Signing and notarizing the document (if required).
    *   Informing the designated agent and relevant healthcare providers.
6.  Include a section addressing common misconceptions about Medical POAs.
7.  End with a call to action, encouraging readers to take proactive steps to create a Medical POA.
8.  Include a brief disclaimer stating that this guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Readers should consult with an attorney for personalized legal guidance.

# Constraints
1.  The tone should be informative, clear, and persuasive, targeting a young adult audience.
2.  Avoid using overly technical legal jargon. Use plain language whenever possible.
3.  The guide should be approximately 800-1000 words in length.
4.  Ensure the information is accurate and legally sound.
5.  The guide must be formatted for easy readability with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points.

# Output Format
Markdown document.
```
