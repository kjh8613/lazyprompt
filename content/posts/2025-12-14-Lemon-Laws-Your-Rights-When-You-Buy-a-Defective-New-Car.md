This prompt generates a consumer protection guide about US Lemon Laws, explaining the criteria for obtaining a refund or replacement vehicle due to defects. This guide will help consumers understand their rights and navigate the legal process when buying a defective new car.

```markdown
# Role
Consumer Rights Advocate & Legal Expert

# Context
You are a consumer rights advocate with extensive knowledge of US Lemon Laws. You are tasked with creating a clear and informative guide for consumers who have purchased a new vehicle with significant defects. This guide will explain their rights and the steps they need to take to pursue a claim.

# Task
Write a consumer protection guide titled "Lemon Laws: Your Rights When You Buy a Defective New Car." The guide must include the following sections:

1.  **Introduction:** Briefly explain what Lemon Laws are and their purpose.
2.  **What Vehicles Are Covered?** Define which types of vehicles are typically covered under Lemon Laws (e.g., new cars, trucks, SUVs, motorcycles; leased vehicles). Mention exclusions, if any (e.g., used cars, commercial vehicles).
3.  **What Qualifies as a "Lemon"?** Detail the criteria a vehicle must meet to be considered a lemon. Include specific examples of "substantial defects" (e.g., engine problems, brake failures, electrical issues). Explain the importance of documentation and repair records.
4.  **Repair Attempt Requirements:** Explain the repair attempt requirements that must be met before a buyer can claim a lemon. For example, specify criteria like:
    *   The number of repair attempts for the *same* defect (e.g., "3 or 4 attempts").
    *   The number of *total* days the vehicle has been out of service for repairs (e.g., "30 days").
    *   Whether the defect persists *after* these attempts.
5.  **Manufacturer's Responsibilities:** Describe what responsibilities the manufacturer has under Lemon Laws, including providing a refund or a replacement vehicle. Clarify what a "full refund" typically entails (e.g., purchase price, taxes, registration fees).
6.  **How to Pursue a Lemon Law Claim:** Outline the steps a buyer should take to pursue a claim, including:
    *   Notifying the manufacturer of the defect and repair history.
    *   Gathering documentation (purchase agreement, repair orders, warranty information).
    *   Considering arbitration or legal action.
7.  **State-Specific Variations:** Briefly mention that Lemon Laws vary by state, and it's important to consult with an attorney in their specific state.
8.  **Conclusion:** Reiterate the importance of knowing one's rights when purchasing a new vehicle.

# Constraints
*   The guide should be written in clear, concise, and easy-to-understand language, avoiding legal jargon where possible.
*   The guide should be informative and empowering for consumers.
*   Focus on general principles of US Lemon Laws, acknowledging state-specific variations.
*   The tone should be professional and authoritative.
*   The guide should be no more than 800 words.

# Output Format
Markdown document with the title "Lemon Laws: Your Rights When You Buy a Defective New Car" and the sections listed above, formatted with appropriate headings and subheadings. Use bullet points where appropriate for clarity.
```
