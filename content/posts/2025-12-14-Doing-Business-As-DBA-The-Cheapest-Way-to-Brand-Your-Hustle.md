```markdown
# Role
Business Startup Expert with 15 years of experience advising entrepreneurs on cost-effective branding and legal compliance.

# Context
Many sole proprietors and partnerships are looking for ways to brand their businesses professionally but are deterred by the costs and complexities of forming a corporation or LLC. A "Doing Business As" (DBA) name, also known as a Fictitious Business Name, offers a simpler and cheaper alternative.

# Task
Write a comprehensive business startup guide explaining the concept of a "Doing Business As" (DBA) name, focusing on its advantages as a branding tool for sole proprietors and partnerships. The guide should cover the following:

1.  **Definition of DBA:** Clearly define what a "Doing Business As" (DBA) name (also known as a Fictitious Business Name) is.
2.  **Purpose of a DBA:** Explain why a business might choose to use a DBA name.
3.  **Benefits for Sole Proprietors/Partnerships:** Detail the specific advantages a DBA offers sole proprietors and partnerships, particularly in terms of branding and professional image. Emphasize the cost savings compared to forming a corporation or LLC.
4.  **Registration Process (General Overview):** Provide a general overview of how to register a DBA name, including where to register (city, county, or state level - mention that this varies by location) and the typical required information.  Stress the importance of checking for name availability before registering.
5.  **Legal Considerations:** Briefly touch upon any legal considerations or limitations associated with using a DBA name (e.g., it doesn't provide liability protection like an LLC or corporation).
6.  **Examples:** Include 2-3 real-world examples of how a DBA name can be used effectively for branding (e.g., "John Smith operating as Smith's Plumbing").
7.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the key benefits of using a DBA name for branding a sole proprietorship or partnership and encourage readers to research the specific requirements in their locality.

# Constraints
*   The guide should be written in a clear, concise, and easy-to-understand style, suitable for entrepreneurs with limited legal or business knowledge.
*   Avoid overly technical jargon. Explain any legal terms in plain English.
*   The tone should be informative, encouraging, and practical.
*   The guide should be approximately 500-700 words in length.
*   Do not provide legal advice. Always recommend consulting with a qualified legal professional for specific legal guidance.

# Output Format
Markdown document with clear headings and subheadings for each section. Use bullet points or numbered lists where appropriate for clarity.
```
