```markdown
# Role
Senior Financial Advisor and Experienced Family Law Attorney specializing in "Grey Divorce" cases.

# Context
Many individuals are divorcing later in life (after 50), often referred to as "grey divorce." This presents unique financial challenges compared to divorces at younger ages due to factors like accumulated assets, retirement accounts, and potential long-term impact on financial security during retirement. The target audience is individuals considering or currently going through a divorce after 50.

# Task
Write a comprehensive and informative blog post titled "Navigating the 'Grey Divorce' Financial Landscape: Protecting Your Assets and Retirement." The blog post must address the following:

1.  **Introduction:** Briefly define "grey divorce" and highlight its unique financial implications. Emphasize the importance of financial planning during this process.
2.  **Dividing Retirement Accounts:**
    *   Explain how 401(k)s and IRAs are typically divided in a divorce using Qualified Domestic Relations Orders (QDROs).
    *   Provide examples of how a QDRO works and the potential tax implications.
    *   Mention the importance of getting a qualified professional to draft the QDRO.
3.  **Social Security Benefits:**
    *   Explain how a divorced spouse may be eligible to receive Social Security benefits based on their ex-spouse's earnings record.
    *   Outline the eligibility requirements (e.g., marriage duration, current marital status).
    *   Provide a hypothetical scenario illustrating how Social Security benefits might work in a grey divorce situation.
4.  **Spousal Support (Alimony) Considerations:**
    *   Discuss the factors courts consider when determining spousal support in a grey divorce (e.g., length of marriage, earning potential, health).
    *   Explain the different types of spousal support (e.g., rehabilitative, permanent).
    *   Specifically address how spousal support laws vary across **California, Texas, Florida, and New York**.
5.  **Protecting Pre-Marital Assets:**
    *   Explain how to protect pre-marital assets during a divorce.
    *   Discuss the importance of tracing assets and maintaining proper documentation.
    *   Mention the role of prenuptial and postnuptial agreements.
6.  **Estate Planning Implications:**
    *   Explain how divorce impacts estate planning documents (e.g., wills, trusts, power of attorney).
    *   Emphasize the need to update these documents after a divorce is finalized.
    *   Highlight the importance of beneficiary designations on retirement accounts and life insurance policies.
7.  **Actionable Tips to Mitigate Financial Losses:**
    *   Provide a bulleted list of at least 5 actionable tips for individuals going through a grey divorce to protect their finances and secure their retirement. Examples:
        *   Hire a qualified financial advisor and divorce attorney.
        *   Gather all financial documents.
        *   Understand your state's divorce laws.
        *   Don't make emotional decisions.
        *   Consider mediation.
8.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the key financial considerations in a grey divorce and reiterate the importance of seeking professional guidance.
9.  **Resources:** Include links to relevant legal and financial resources (e.g., IRS publications, state bar associations, financial planning organizations).

# Constraints
*   The blog post should be written in a professional, informative, and empathetic tone.
*   The content must be accurate and legally sound. Consult reputable sources for legal and financial information.
*   The blog post should be approximately 1200-1500 words in length.
*   Avoid using overly technical jargon. Explain complex financial concepts in plain language.
*   The blog post must be optimized for readability with clear headings, subheadings, and bullet points.

# Output Format
Markdown format. Include a clear title, introduction, body paragraphs organized by the tasks specified above, a conclusion, and a resources section with relevant links. Use appropriate Markdown formatting for headings, lists, and emphasis.
```
