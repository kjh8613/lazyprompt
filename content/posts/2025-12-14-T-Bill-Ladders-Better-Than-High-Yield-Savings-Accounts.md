```markdown
# Role
Financial Analyst specializing in fixed-income investments with a focus on US Treasury securities.

# Context
Many individuals are looking for safe and liquid investment options that offer a competitive yield compared to traditional savings accounts. US Treasury Bills (T-Bills) are a popular choice, and a "ladder" strategy can optimize returns while maintaining access to funds.

# Task
Write a comprehensive, yet easy-to-understand investment guide titled: "T-Bill Ladders: Better Than High-Yield Savings Accounts?". The guide should cover the following points:

1.  **Introduction:** Briefly explain what US Treasury Bills (T-Bills) are, emphasizing their safety (backed by the US government) and how they work (discounted purchase, face value at maturity).
2.  **Understanding T-Bill Yields:** Explain how T-Bill yields are determined, focusing on the auction process and factors influencing interest rates (e.g., Federal Reserve policy). Define key terms like "discount rate" and "annualized yield."
3.  **What is a T-Bill Ladder?:** Define a T-Bill ladder strategy and explain its benefits:
    *   Higher potential yields compared to holding cash.
    *   Regular access to funds as bills mature.
    *   Flexibility to reinvest at current rates.
    *   Mitigation of interest rate risk.
4.  **Building a T-Bill Ladder:** Provide a step-by-step guide on how to build a ladder using 4-week, 8-week, and 13-week T-Bills. Include a practical example with hypothetical dollar amounts and interest rates. The example should illustrate how to roll over matured bills into new purchases to maintain the ladder.
5.  **T-Bill Ladder vs. High-Yield Savings Accounts:** Compare and contrast T-Bill ladders with High-Yield Savings Accounts (HYSAs). Discuss the pros and cons of each:
    *   **T-Bill Ladder Pros:** Potentially higher yields, state and local tax exemption.
    *   **T-Bill Ladder Cons:** Requires more active management, potential for yield fluctuations.
    *   **HYSA Pros:** Simpler, FDIC insured (up to $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank).
    *   **HYSA Cons:** Lower yields, subject to state and local taxes.
6.  **Where to Buy T-Bills:** Explain how to purchase T-Bills directly from the U.S. Treasury through TreasuryDirect.gov. Mention brokerages as an alternative, noting potential fees.
7.  **Tax Implications:** Briefly explain the tax treatment of T-Bills (federal income tax only; exempt from state and local taxes).
8.  **Risks to Consider:** Mention the primary risks associated with T-Bills:
    *   **Reinvestment Risk:** The risk that interest rates will be lower when bills mature and are reinvested.
    *   **Inflation Risk:** The risk that inflation will erode the real return on investment.
    *   **Liquidity Risk (minimal):** Although T-Bills are liquid, selling them before maturity may result in a loss.
9.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the benefits of a T-Bill ladder strategy as a conservative investment option, emphasizing the importance of considering individual financial circumstances and risk tolerance.

# Constraints
*   The tone should be informative, conservative, and approachable for a general audience with limited financial knowledge.
*   Avoid overly technical jargon. If technical terms are necessary, define them clearly.
*   Focus on practical advice and actionable steps.
*   The guide should be approximately 800-1200 words in length.
*   Do not provide specific financial advice. The guide should be for informational purposes only.

# Output Format
The output should be a well-structured article in Markdown format, suitable for publication on a personal finance blog. Use headings and subheadings to organize the content logically. Include a disclaimer stating that the information is for educational purposes only and not financial advice.
```
