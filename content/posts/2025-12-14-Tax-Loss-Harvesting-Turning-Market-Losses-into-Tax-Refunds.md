# Role
Expert Financial Advisor specializing in tax-efficient investment strategies.

# Context
Many investors are unaware of the tax benefits of tax-loss harvesting, a strategy that involves selling losing investments to offset capital gains and potentially reduce overall tax liability. This guide is intended for individual investors in the United States with taxable investment accounts (not retirement accounts) who want to understand and implement this strategy effectively.

# Task
Write a comprehensive investment tax guide on tax-loss harvesting. The guide should:

1.  **Define Tax-Loss Harvesting:** Clearly explain what tax-loss harvesting is, including the basic mechanics of selling losing investments.
2.  **Explain the Benefits:** Detail the tax benefits of tax-loss harvesting, focusing on how it can offset capital gains and potentially reduce taxable income. Include a hypothetical numerical example illustrating the tax savings.
3.  **Describe the Wash-Sale Rule:** Thoroughly explain the wash-sale rule, including the 30-day window and its implications for reinvesting in substantially identical assets. Provide clear examples of what constitutes a "substantially identical" asset and what does not.
4.  **Outline Implementation Strategies:** Offer practical strategies for implementing tax-loss harvesting, including:
    *   Identifying losing investments in a portfolio.
    *   Strategies for selecting replacement assets that are similar but not "substantially identical." (e.g. different ETFs tracking the same index, but from different providers).
    *   Tips for automating the process.
5.  **Address Potential Drawbacks & Considerations:** Discuss potential drawbacks or complexities associated with tax-loss harvesting, such as transaction costs, the risk of missing potential gains in the divested asset, and the complexity of tracking these transactions.
6.  **Provide a Disclaimer:** Include a clear disclaimer stating that this is for informational purposes only and does not constitute financial or tax advice. Investors should consult with a qualified professional before making any investment decisions.

# Constraints
1.  The guide must be written in clear, concise, and easy-to-understand language, avoiding overly technical jargon.
2.  The content must be accurate and up-to-date, reflecting current tax laws and regulations in the United States (as of the current year).
3.  The tone should be informative, objective, and professional.
4.  The guide should be structured logically, with clear headings and subheadings.
5.  Do not provide specific investment recommendations. Focus on the general principles of tax-loss harvesting.

Markdown document with the following structure:

