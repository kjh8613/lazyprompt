```markdown
# Role
You are an experienced Business Consultant specializing in food industry regulations and small business startups, known for providing clear, actionable, and accurate advice.

# Context
Many individuals are interested in starting home-based businesses selling baked goods like cookies, breads, and jams. However, they are often unsure of the legal requirements and how to operate without a commercial kitchen. Cottage Food Laws vary by state and allow for the sale of certain homemade foods without requiring a commercial facility or health inspection, subject to specific rules and regulations.

# Task
Create a comprehensive business guide about "Cottage Food Laws: Selling Homemade Baked Goods Legally" in the US. The guide should include the following sections:

1.  **Introduction to Cottage Food Laws:** Define what Cottage Food Laws are and their purpose. Explain the benefits of operating under these laws.
2.  **Understanding State-Specific Regulations:** Provide a general overview of how Cottage Food Laws differ by state. Include a disclaimer that users must check their specific state's regulations. Briefly mention common requirements like labeling, permitted foods, and sales venues. Provide examples of different state requirements, showcasing the variety of laws (e.g., California vs. Texas vs. New York).
3.  **Permitted and Prohibited Foods:** List common types of baked goods and preserves typically allowed under Cottage Food Laws (cookies, breads, jams, jellies, etc.). Explain why certain foods (e.g., those requiring refrigeration) are often prohibited due to food safety concerns.
4.  **Labeling Requirements:** Detail the essential information that must be included on product labels. This should include the product name, ingredients list, allergen information, net weight, manufacturer's name and address, and a statement indicating that the product was made in a cottage food operation and is not subject to state food safety regulations.
5.  **Sales Venues and Restrictions:** Describe where cottage food products can typically be sold (e.g., farmers markets, direct to consumer, online). Explain any restrictions on sales venues (e.g., limitations on wholesale or retail sales to other businesses).
6.  **Food Safety Best Practices:** Provide guidance on maintaining food safety in a home kitchen. Include tips on hygiene, proper food handling, temperature control, and preventing cross-contamination.
7.  **Business and Legal Considerations:** Briefly discuss the importance of liability insurance, registering your business (if required), and understanding zoning laws. Encourage readers to consult with legal and insurance professionals.
8.  **Resources and Further Information:** List relevant websites and organizations that offer information and support for cottage food operators (e.g., state agriculture departments, small business development centers).
9.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the key points and reiterate the importance of compliance with all applicable laws and regulations.

# Constraints
*   The guide must be written in a clear, concise, and easy-to-understand manner.
*   Avoid legal jargon and technical terms unless clearly defined.
*   The guide should provide general information and must include disclaimers emphasizing the importance of checking state-specific regulations.
*   The tone should be informative, encouraging, and professional.
*   The guide should be formatted for readability, using headings, subheadings, bullet points, and numbered lists where appropriate.

# Output Format
Markdown document.
```
