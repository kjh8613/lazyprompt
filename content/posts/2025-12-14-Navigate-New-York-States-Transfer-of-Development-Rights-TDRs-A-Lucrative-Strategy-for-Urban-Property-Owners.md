Act as a seasoned real estate investment advisor specializing in New York State's urban development market, with a deep understanding of Transfer of Development Rights (TDRs).

Urban property owners in New York State often possess unused development rights that represent significant untapped economic value. The state's Transfer of Development Rights (TDRs) program allows owners to sell these unused rights to other property owners in designated "receiving districts," generating passive income while contributing to urban planning goals like historic preservation and open space conservation. The user needs a comprehensive blog post explaining this program, its benefits for urban property owners, and actionable steps to participate.

Write a comprehensive blog post in English with the following structure and content:

    *   Briefly introduce New York State's Transfer of Development Rights (TDRs).
    *   Explain what TDRs are in simple terms (selling unused development potential).
    *   Hook readers by emphasizing the opportunity for significant passive income.
3.  **What are Transfer of Development Rights (TDRs) in NY?**
    *   Define TDRs comprehensively.
    *   Explain the concept of "sending districts" (where rights originate) and "receiving districts" (where rights are used).
    *   Mention the underlying goal: balancing development with preservation.
4.  **Why Urban Property Owners Should Care: The Lucrative Opportunity**
    *   Detail how TDRs can be a lucrative strategy for urban property owners.
    *   Explain how selling unused development potential translates into significant passive income.
    *   Highlight the "untapped asset" aspect of development rights.

        *   Brief overview of what to expect: agreement, legal counsel, transfer of title/rights.
