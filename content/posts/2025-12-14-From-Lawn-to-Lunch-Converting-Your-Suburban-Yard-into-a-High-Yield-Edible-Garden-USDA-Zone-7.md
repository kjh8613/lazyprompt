# Role
Expert Horticulturalist and Financial Analyst specializing in sustainable gardening practices.

# Context
Many homeowners in USDA plant hardiness zone 7 are looking for ways to reduce their environmental impact, save money, and eat healthier. Converting a traditional lawn into a productive edible garden is a popular option, but requires careful planning and execution to be successful. This blog post will provide a comprehensive guide for homeowners in zone 7 who are interested in making this conversion.

# Task
Write a detailed blog post titled "From Lawn to Lunch: Converting Your Suburban Yard into a High-Yield Edible Garden (USDA Zone 7)". The blog post should include the following sections:

1.  **Introduction:** Briefly introduce the concept of converting lawns into edible gardens and highlight the benefits (environmental, financial, health). Include a compelling opening paragraph.
2.  **Understanding USDA Zone 7:** Explain the characteristics of USDA zone 7, including typical temperature ranges and growing season length. Briefly mention suitable microclimates within Zone 7.
3.  **Step-by-Step Guide to Soil Preparation:**
    *   Describe how to remove the existing lawn (e.g., sheet mulching, sod removal).
    *   Explain how to test the soil pH and amend it if necessary.
    *   Discuss the importance of adding compost and other organic matter.
    *   Provide specific instructions for creating raised beds or in-ground gardens.
4.  **Selecting Appropriate Vegetables, Fruits, and Herbs for Zone 7:**
    *   List recommended vegetables (e.g., tomatoes, peppers, beans, squash, lettuce, spinach, kale) and their optimal planting times. Include specific varieties known to thrive in Zone 7.
    *   List recommended fruits (e.g., berries, fruit trees) and their specific needs, including chill hours for fruit trees.
    *   List recommended herbs (e.g., basil, rosemary, thyme, oregano) and their preferred growing conditions.
5.  **Companion Planting Techniques:**
    *   Explain the concept of companion planting.
    *   Provide specific examples of beneficial plant pairings for Zone 7 gardens (e.g., tomatoes and basil, carrots and onions, squash and nasturtiums).
6.  **Water Conservation Strategies:**
    *   Discuss the importance of water conservation in the garden.
    *   Explain how to use drip irrigation effectively.
    *   Recommend mulching techniques to retain soil moisture.
    *   Mention rainwater harvesting as a supplemental water source.
7.  **Pest Control Methods:**
    *   Emphasize the importance of organic and Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies.
    *   Describe common garden pests in Zone 7 and how to identify them.
    *   Recommend specific organic pest control methods (e.g., beneficial insects, neem oil, row covers).
8.  **Cost-Benefit Analysis:**
    *   Compare the costs of maintaining a traditional lawn (e.g., mowing, fertilizing, watering) to the costs of establishing and maintaining an edible garden (e.g., soil amendments, seeds, irrigation).
    *   Estimate the potential savings from growing your own food, including the market value of the produce.
    *   Discuss the potential for selling excess produce at farmers' markets or to local restaurants.
9.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the benefits of converting a lawn into an edible garden and encourage readers to take action.

**Throughout the blog post, include specific, actionable advice and recommendations for readers in USDA zone 7.**

# Constraints
