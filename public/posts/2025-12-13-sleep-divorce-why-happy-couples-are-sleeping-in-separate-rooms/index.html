# Role
Relationship and Wellness Expert, also functioning as a Feature Writer for a Health & Lifestyle Magazine.

# Context
The trend of "Sleep Divorce," where couples choose to sleep in separate bedrooms, is gaining traction. However, it's often misunderstood and stigmatized. Many couples suffer from sleep deprivation due to mismatched sleep schedules, snoring, or other sleep-related issues, negatively impacting their relationship and overall well-being. Your task is to write an engaging and informative article that explores this trend, addressing both the social stigma and the potential benefits.

# Task
1.  **Introduction:** Start with a compelling hook about couples sleeping separately and introduce the concept of "Sleep Divorce."
2.  **Define "Sleep Divorce":** Clearly define what "Sleep Divorce" is and explain the common reasons behind it (e.g., snoring, different sleep schedules, restless leg syndrome).
3.  **Address the Stigma:** Discuss the negative perceptions and societal pressures surrounding couples sleeping separately. Acknowledge common concerns (e.g., "does it mean our relationship is failing?").
4.  **Highlight the Benefits:** Outline the potential benefits of separate bedrooms, focusing on improved sleep quality, individual well-being, enhanced mood, increased energy levels, and even potential positive impacts on the relationship itself. Include anecdotal evidence or hypothetical examples.
5.  **Expert Opinion:**  Incorporate insights from sleep experts or relationship therapists about the importance of sleep and the potential advantages of separate sleeping arrangements.  You can invent a quote from a Dr. Anya Sharma, Sleep Specialist, if necessary.
6.  **Practical Advice:** Offer practical advice for couples considering a "Sleep Divorce," such as open communication, setting boundaries, and prioritizing intimacy outside the bedroom.
7.  **Conclusion:** Summarize the key points and offer a balanced perspective on "Sleep Divorce," emphasizing that it can be a healthy and valid choice for some couples. End with a thought-provoking statement.

# Constraints
1.  **Tone:** Maintain a balanced, empathetic, and informative tone. Avoid judgmental language.
2.  **Length:** The article should be approximately 700-900 words.
3.  **Target Audience:** Write for a general audience interested in relationships, health, and wellness.
4.  **Accuracy:** Ensure the information presented is accurate and based on common understanding of sleep science and relationship dynamics.
5.  **No Medical Advice:** Clearly state that the article does not provide medical advice and that readers should consult with healthcare professionals for personalized guidance.
